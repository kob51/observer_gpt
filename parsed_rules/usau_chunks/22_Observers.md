## 22. Observers

<!-- section: 22 source: usau -->

### 22.A. Observers may be used if desired by the captains or the event organizer.
<!-- rule: 22.A source: usau -->

### 22.B. Observers may perform any or all of the following duties:
<!-- rule: 22.B source: usau -->

    - **22.B.1.** Track time limits and announce associated warnings and expirations.
      <!-- rule: 22.B.1 source: usau -->
    - **22.B.2.** Resolve player disputes.
      <!-- rule: 22.B.2 source: usau -->
      - **22.B.2.a.** Any player directly involved in a dispute may request observer resolution.
        <!-- rule: 22.B.2.a source: usau -->
      - **22.B.2.b.** An observer may resolve a dispute without request from the players
        <!-- rule: 22.B.2.b source: usau -->
involved if they cannot resolve it in a timely manner.
      - **22.B.2.c.** If an observer is involved in resolving a dispute, play restarts with a check.
        <!-- rule: 22.B.2.c source: usau -->
    - **22.B.3.** Censure or eject players for Spirit of the Game infractions. This includes assigning
      <!-- rule: 22.B.3 source: usau -->
responsibility for game delays to a specific player.
    - **22.B.4.** Render opinions on other on-field events (e.g., line and offside calls), as determined
      <!-- rule: 22.B.4 source: usau -->
in advance by the event organizer.
### 22.C. By playing under observers, the players agree to abide by the observers' decisions.
<!-- rule: 22.C source: usau -->

### 22.D. Players may overrule an observer's active ruling, provided it is to their team's detriment. Players
<!-- rule: 22.D source: usau -->

may also decline enforcement of a yardage penalty assessed through the misconduct system.
Observers may deny the declination if they feel the teams are trying to circumvent mandatory
tournament rules, guidelines, or player safety.
